Extracted Contents Compilation (2024–2026)
TL;DR
The GEMS 2025 Scheme Rules and their registered Annexures A–G were located at their
exact gems.gov.za URLs, but Annexures B (contributions) and C (per-option benefits)
are scanned image PDFs with NO machine-readable text; their full rand contents were
reconstructed from the text-rich 2025 Benefit Guide (mirrored on medicalaid.com) and
the member/provider guides, which were fully extracted.
Complete contents were extracted for: the full per-option 2025 benefit grids (Tanzanite
One, Beryl, Ruby, Emerald, Emerald Value, Onyx), the 2025 Chronic Medicine Guide
(CDL/ACDL per option + MAC legend), the 2025 Oncology Guide (rand limits per option
+ appeals ladder), the 2025 Underwriting/LJP Guide (s29A rules + penalty bands), the
HIV DMP form, dental/optometry rules, and provider tariff-modifier/RMR rules.
The premise of a granular per-procedure rand co-payment schedule (arthroscopy = Rx,
cataract = Ry, etc.) is NOT supported by GEMS’s published documents: GEMS uses a
flat co-payment model (R1,000 and R15,000 triggers). Per-procedure surgery is
governed by annual surgical/prostheses limits and pre-authorisation.
Key Findings
Scheme scale (context)
GEMS is the largest restricted-membership medical scheme in South Africa; per IOL (19 Nov
2025), it has “over 890 000 principal members and more than 2.4 million beneficiaries.”
(The 2025 Emerald marketing brochure cites “over 855 000 principal members and over 2,3
million beneficiaries” — figures vary slightly by document date.)
Document repository structure
GEMS publishes its documents under gems.gov.za/-/media/Project/Documents/... paths,
indexed from these landing pages: Scheme Rules ( /Information/Scheme-Rules ), Member
Guides ( /Information/Member-Guides ), Provider Guides ( /Healthcare-
Providers/Provider-Guides ), Forms, Formulary Lists, Tariff Files, and the Provider FAQ.
Archives run 2020–2025 for Rules.

3. CO-PAYMENTS — FLAT MODEL (subagent-confirmed; NOT a per-
procedure schedule)
GEMS does not publish a granular per-procedure rand co-payment table. Confirmed co-
payments (verbatim sources):
R1,000 — elective gastroscopy/colonoscopy in an acute hospital GEMS Medical Aid
(introduced for 2026). gems Per GEMS 2026 benefit changes: “A co-payment of
R1000 has been introduced for all elective gastroscopies and/or colonoscopies
performed in acute hospitals.”
R1,000 per admission — if a private-hospital admission is not pre-authorised at
least 48 hours in advance (or an emergency admission is not notified within one
working day). Per GEMS official FAQ: “Unless it is an emergency, a co-payment of R1
000 per admission will apply if pre-authorisation is not obtained within 48 hours of the
event… the Scheme must be notified within one working day of the admission.”
R15,000 — voluntary/elective use of a non-network hospital on Tanzanite One and
Emerald Value (and Beryl per 2026 changes; also non-network maternity from 2026).
Per GEMS Dental Provider Guide 2026: “Members need to use state or GEMS network
hospitals for all admissions. The voluntary use of a non-network private hospital will
result in a co-payment of up to R15 000.”
30% — non-DSP co-payment (chronic medicine from non-nominated pharmacy after
first claim; gems specialist consult without FP referral on Tanzanite One/EVO).

11. CMS PMB REFERENCE MATERIAL
PMB structure: emergencies + 271 Diagnosis-Treatment Pairs (defined in Annexure A of
the General Regulations) + 26/27 CDL chronic conditions. Per CMS
(medicalschemes.co.za): “The 271 conditions that qualify for PMB cover are diagnosis-
specific and include a range of ailments that can be divided into 15 broad categories”;
the CDL “specifies medication and treatment for the 26 chronic conditions.”
CMS “PMB ICD-10 Coded List” is downloadable from medicalschemes.com (an .xlsx
PMB ICD-10 coded list exists; the “2022 PMB ICD-10 Coded list” is the most recent
version identified on medicalschemes.co.za), representing CMS’s interpretation of the
“diagnosis” portion of the PMBs. Dagger/asterisk code logic applies; in conflict, the
regulation definitions prevail. Third-party consolidations of the 271 DTPs + 27 CDL are
published by Discovery.
Recommendations
1. For the master document, treat the text-extractable guides as the primary source
of rand values and cite the registered Annexures B/C as the legal authority — but flag
that they must be OCR’d from the image PDFs to verify. The 2025 Benefit Guide values
above match Annexure C by design.
2. OCR these specific URLs to capture Annexure B (contribution tables per income band
+ LJP) and Annexure C (each option): the six d–i Annexure C PDFs and the c
Annexure B PDF on the 2025 Scheme Rules page. Budget for image OCR; text extraction
returns empty.
3. Drop the per-procedure co-payment schedule as a structural element — it does not
exist at GEMS. Replace with the flat co-payment model (R1,000 / R15,000 / 30% non-
DSP) plus the annual surgical/prostheses limits per option. If a client insists on per-
procedure figures, they are likely conflating GEMS with Discovery/Bonitas, which do
publish such lists.

Caveats
The single most important limitation: the legally registered Annexures B and C (2025)
are scanned image PDFs and returned no machine-readable text; all rand benefit values
were sourced from the text-extractable 2025 Benefit Guide (hosted on medicalaid.com,
a third-party aggregator that mirrors the official GEMS guide) and cross-checked
against member/provider guides. Verify against OCR of the registered PDFs before
treating as legally definitive.
Some minor internal inconsistencies exist in the source Benefit Guide (e.g. wheelchair
“R7,761” vs “R7,716” in the Beryl block GEMS Medical Aid — likely a typo for R7,716).
The FP Provider Guide 2025 PDF and the campaign “What’s New” PDFs are robots-
disallowed to automated fetch; their contents were captured from search-engine
snippets and the Provider FAQ, not a full-document extraction.
The HIV Member Guide 2025 PDF returned 404/robots errors at the URLs tried; the
DMP application form was fully captured, and the programme rules were captured from
the Managing-HIV-AIDS page. The full 2025 HIV guide PDF should be re-attempted
